\chapter{Literature Review}
\label{literature_review}
\thispagestyle{empty}


This chapter presents a comprehensive review of the existing literature related to your research topic. The literature review should:

\begin{itemize}
    \item Identify key theories, concepts, and frameworks
    \item Summarize previous research findings
    \item Identify gaps in the current knowledge
    \item Justify the need for your research
\end{itemize}

\section{Theoretical Framework}

Describe the theoretical framework that underpins your research. Explain the key theories and concepts that guide your study.

\subsection{Key Concepts}

Define and explain the main concepts used in your research. For example:

\textbf{Concept 1:} Definition and explanation of the first key concept.

\textbf{Concept 2:} Definition and explanation of the second key concept.

\subsection{Theoretical Models}

Discuss relevant theoretical models that inform your research approach.

\section{Previous Research}

Review and synthesize previous research in your field. Organize this section thematically or chronologically, depending on what makes most sense for your topic.

\subsection{Research Area 1}

Discuss research related to the first major area of your study. Cite relevant sources and explain their findings.

\subsection{Research Area 2}

Discuss research related to the second major area of your study.

\section{Research Gap}

Based on your literature review, identify the gaps in current knowledge that your research aims to address. Explain how your work will contribute to filling these gaps.

\section{Summary}

Summarize the key points from your literature review and explain how they lead to your research questions or hypotheses.
